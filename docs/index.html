Abstract

High-quality 3D neuron segmentation is critical for neuroscience, but densely labeled volumetric data are scarce and expensive to obtain. In contrast, large-scale 2D datasets enable powerful self-supervised models such as DINOv3. We present NeurINO, a framework that transfers 2D ConvNeXt-based DINOv3 visual priors to 3D neuron segmentation by inflating kernels into a MedNeXt-style architecture and carefully aligning normalization and feature statistics. Experiments on multiple neuron datasets show consistent gains in segmentation quality and thin-structure recall, especially in low-label regimes.

Method

NeurINO keeps the overall encoder–decoder design simple: a DINOv3 ConvNeXt encoder inflated into 3D, followed by a MedNeXt-style decoder tailored for neuron morphology.

Framework. 2D DINOv3 ConvNeXt stages are inflated into 3D and plugged into a MedNeXt-style decoder for volumetric neuron segmentation.

Results

NeurINO improves over strong 3D baselines on several neuron datasets, with higher Dice scores and better preservation of thin neurites. The transfer of DINOv3 priors makes the model notably more label-efficient.

Visual results. Compared with baseline 3D models, NeurINO reduces merge/split errors and preserves fine neuron branches.
