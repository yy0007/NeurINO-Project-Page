NeurINO transfers 2D self-supervised DINOv3 priors into a 3D segmentation framework, enabling accurate neuron segmentation under limited 3D labels.

Abstract

High-quality 3D neuron segmentation is crucial for understanding neural circuits, but collecting densely annotated volumetric data is extremely expensive. In contrast, large-scale 2D natural-image datasets are abundant and have been successfully leveraged by powerful self-supervised models such as DINOv3.

We propose NeurINO, a 3D neuron segmentation framework that transfers 2D DINOv3 visual priors into a volumetric MedNeXt-style architecture. By inflating ConvNeXt-based DINOv3 weights into 3D and carefully designing cross-dimensional initialization and normalization strategies, NeurINO effectively bridges the 2D–3D domain gap. Experiments on multiple neuron datasets demonstrate that NeurINO substantially improves both segmentation quality and training stability over strong 3D baselines.

Method

NeurINO is built on a 3D MedNeXt-style U-Net with a DINOv3 ConvNeXt encoder. We:

  • Inflate 2D ConvNeXt blocks into 3D while preserving receptive fields and feature distributions.
  • Introduce stage-wise 2D-to-3D kernel inflation strategies (center/average inflation).
  • Design DINO-aware bottlenecks and decoder blocks tailored for neuron morphology.
  • Adopt normalization replacement and optional skeleton-aware objectives to refine fine structures.
